Studio Kiln crafted a dynamic brand identity for Fellowkids, an agency at the intersection of esports and entertainment. Addressing their expanded scope, the design strategically embodies authentic fandom. A versatile 'Fellowkid' character, conceptualized as a collectible art toy, anchors the visual narrative, enabling responsive community engagement. This is complemented by a vibrant, Y2K-inspired aesthetic and contemporary typography, resulting in a sophisticated, adaptive system that resonates authentically with its audience and truly behaves like a fan.

## Full Content

In the dynamic landscape of modern entertainment, where digital communities thrive and allegiances are passionately forged, Fellowkids stands as an agency dedicated to building authentic fandoms. As their influence grew, however, their own brand identity began to feel outpaced, struggling to encapsulate their unique blend of serious playfulness and anti-corporate edge. The challenge for Studio Kiln was to craft an identity that not only matched Fellowkids' escalating calibre of work but authentically embodied the very spirit of fandom itself – responsive, adaptable, and deeply engaged with community culture, without succumbing to inauthentic "corporate cosplay."

Studio Kiln’s creative solution centered on a deceptively simple yet profound insight: if Fellowkids builds fandom for others, its brand should *behave* like a fan. This philosophy culminated in the creation of "The Fellowkid"—a hooded, gender-neutral figure designed to be the identity's emotional core. Recognizable across esports, entertainment, and sports, the hoodie serves as a universal symbol of belonging. This distinctive silhouette empowers the Fellowkid to effortlessly shift allegiances, adopting various colors, textures, and graphics to represent diverse teams and moments. Crucially, the concept was first materialized as a physical vinyl art toy, making the brand itself a collectible piece of culture, transforming the abstract promise of fandom into a tangible, interactive reality.

To support this core character, Studio Kiln constructed a vibrant, Y2K-inspired visual world. This aesthetic draws from nostalgic tech cues, featuring translucent plastics, candy-bright hues, saturated neons, and deep blacks. A deliberate palette shift allows the brand to amplify "hype" moments with hot accents while maintaining sophistication and groundedness with softer hardware greys. Typographically, Aeonik provides a critical counterweight, offering clarity and neutrality against the system's bespoke, Y2K-flavoured elements, with Aeonik Mono lending a subtle technical inflection. The entire system is designed as a modular "digital sticker sheet"—an eclectic kit of character icons, custom typography, and decals, anchored by a simple grid, empowering the Fellowkids team to create dynamic, engaging assets with ease.

The impact of this comprehensive rebrand has been immediate and resonant. Fellowkids has noted tangible wins, with new clients specifically commending the redesigned pitch decks for their energetic, clean, and exciting presentation. The vinyl art toy, in particular, has become a potent symbol, celebrated for its playful yet premium, collectible yet accessible nature—a direct reflection of Fellowkids' ethos of taking fandom seriously without taking themselves too seriously. This refreshed identity has not only elevated Fellowkids' external perception to match their ambition but has also unlocked deeper internal confidence, truly living their values, and fostering a brand experience that beckons users to collect, customize, and ultimately, root for Fellowkids.
